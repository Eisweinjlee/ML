0.1 Bayes Estimation

Let first see the Bayes equation: P(w∣x)=p(x∣w)p(w)p(x)P(w|x)=\frac{p(x|w)p(w)}{p(x)}P(w∣x)=p(x)p(x∣w)p(w)​ where p(w)p(w)p(w) is the prior distribution, p(x∣w)p(x|w)p(x∣w) is the conditional probability, and our goal is to get the prosterior P(w∣x)P(w|x)P(w∣x).

It would be easier to understand by an example:

Known: In a park, 1/2 of male are wearing sandals, and 2/3 of female are wearing sandals, and in the park the ratio of male to female is 2:1.

Question: You meet a person wearing sandals, what are the prosiblities the person to be male or female?

Answer:
Assuming x=x=x= wearing sandals, w1=w_1=w1​= male, w2=w_2=w2​= female.

Then we have the prior p(w1)=2/3p(w_1)=2/3p(w1​)=2/3, p(w2)=1/3p(w_2)=1/3p(w2​)=1/3,

the conditional posibilities p(x∣w1)=1/2p(x|w_1)=1/2p(x∣w1​)=1/2, p(x∣w2)=2/3p(x|w_2)=2/3p(x∣w2​)=2/3,

the total posibility of p(x)=p(x∣w1)p(w1)+p(x∣w2)p(w2)=5/9p(x)=p(x|w_1)p(w_1)+p(x|w_2)p(w_2)=5/9p(x)=p(x∣w1​)p(w1​)+p(x∣w2​)p(w2​)=5/9.

Therefore, the prosterior are p(w1∣x)=p(x∣w1)p(w1)p(x)=3/5p(w_1|x)=\frac{p(x|w_1)p(w_1)}{p(x)}=3/5p(w1​∣x)=p(x)p(x∣w1​)p(w1​)​=3/5 p(w2∣x)=p(x∣w2)p(w2)p(x)=2/5p(w_2|x)=\frac{p(x|w_2)p(w_2)}{p(x)}=2/5p(w2​∣x)=p(x)p(x∣w2​)p(w2​)​=2/5

0.2 Problem Introduction

However, in many problems, we are not lucky to get the prior p(wi)p(w_i)p(wi​) and conditional posibilities p(x∣wi)p(x|w_i)p(x∣wi​). In this case, we need to estimate them!

The prior p(wi)p(w_i)p(wi​): We have 3 main ideas to estimate the prior, which would be simple and not our main problems. They are (1) the experience, such as the ratio of gender is close to 1/2, (2) estimating from data, (3) supervised learning.

Conditional posibilities: However, it is hard to have the conditional posiblities p(x∣wi)p(x|w_i)p(x∣wi​). In this case, we can first have a hypothesis and estimate the posibilities via parameters.

0.5 Summary

The general steps for MLE are:

  1. Obtain the likelihood function l(θ)=p(D∣θ)l(\theta)=p(D|\theta)l(θ)=p(D∣θ)

  3. Have the derivative or gradient of H(θ)H(\theta)H(θ)
