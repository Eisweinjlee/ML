0.4 The ERM and SRM

From above derivation, we can apply empirical risk for learning algorithm and this strategy is called Empirical Risk Minimization (ERM).

However, when the dataset is small, ERM would lead to overfitting. We will introduce another strategy, by adding regularization term Ω(ω)\Omega(\omega)Ω(ω), called Strucural Risk Minimization (SRM). h^REG=arg⁡min⁡h∈HRemp(h)+Ω(ω)\hat{h}^{REG}=\arg\min_{h\in\mathcal{H}} R_{emp}(h)+\Omega(\omega)h^REG=argh∈Hmin​Remp​(h)+Ω(ω) where the ω\omegaω is the parameters of h(x)h(x)h(x) and the Ω(ω)\Omega(\omega)Ω(ω) always uses the norm of parameters for regularization, e.g.

  • Square of L2 norm: Ω(ω)=λ∣∣ω∣∣22\Omega(\omega)=\lambda||\omega||_2^2Ω(ω)=λ∣∣ω∣∣22​
  • L1 norm: Ω(ω)=λ∣∣ω∣∣1\Omega(\omega)=\lambda||\omega||_1Ω(ω)=λ∣∣ω∣∣1​
  • L0 norm: Ω(ω)=λ∣∣ω∣∣0=Σ[wi≠0]\Omega(\omega)=\lambda||\omega||_0=\Sigma[w_i=\not 0]Ω(ω)=λ∣∣ω∣∣0​=Σ[wi​≠​0]
